What Is Endometriosis?

Endometriosis is a medical condition caused by the abnormal growth of endometrial cells outside the uterine cavity. The cells lining the inner mucous membrane of a woman’s uterus is known as the endometrium. Endometriosis disorder occurs when the endometrium grows on the bowel, ovaries, and on the tissues lining the pelvis

Symptoms Of Endometriosis

Pelvic Pain

The primary symptom of endometriosis is pelvic pain

Abnormal bleeding

You may experience occasional heavy menstrual periods

Infertility

Endometriosis is sometimes first diagnosed in those seeking treatment for infertility

Causes Of Endometriosis

Menstrual blood flows back to the pelvic cavity via the fallopian tubes during your regular monthly cycle.
The displaced cells are stuck to the pelvic walls of organs like the rectum, ovaries and bladder.
Caesarean delivery leading to the formation of a surgical scar that leads to the growth, thickening and bleeding of endometrial cells during a menstrual cycle.
Cells in the pelvic and belly develop into endometrial cells.
Abdominal growth from embryonic cells may change shape, emulating endometrial cells.
Development of endometrial cells in a foetus.

Why Is It Important To Get Treated Asap?

While endometriosis might be a chronic disorder, there are some effective treatments that can manage fertility issues and pain like medications, surgery, or hormonal therapy, ensuring your quality of life isn’t compromised.

Some of the tests that are prescribed for the diagnosis of endometriosis include:

Analyze Detailed Family History

Your doctor will ask for your family history and evaluate the symptoms of endometriosis within the patient’s family.

Physical Examination

A pelvic examination allows a doctor to manually check the abdomen of a patient for any scars or cyst formation around the uterus.

Ultrasound
Test

An abdominal ultrasound or a transvaginal ultrasound is used to examine the images of the reproductive organs.

Laparoscopy
Test

A small surgical procedure that is done by doctors to directly view and identify endometriosis in patients.
